A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.
I aim to manage my workload in a structured and consistent manner. During the sprints at home, as a baseline I set aside Monday to Friday 8am - 5:30pm (and 1 hours practice Saturday and Sunday) to work through the sprint using the pomodoro timer, setting myself short breaks throughout the day including lunch. If my workload exceeds this allocated time then I will extend my day to meet deadlines. When working with other learners, I want to be open and transparent with how I’m progressing on work and make an effort to be available throughout the day to work though our project together. The more collaboration with other learners the better, I’m really looking forward to peer programming. When things build up and get to me, I will communicate my thoughts to my team or facilitators. Being honest and upfront about your problem is the best solution and even if it’s not solved immediately ‘a trouble shared is a trouble halved’. When I’m experiencing a block there are several ways I go about solving it: Take a short break Google your problem Ask the cohort / team member Ask a the appropriate discord channel Ask a facilitator

Expectations from the facilitation team: Being supportive, patient, and providing honest feedback to develop my technical and non-technical skills.

Scheduling information: I have no current commitments, however I will begin to block out small windows throughout my week so as the work ramps up I have allocated time to leave my computer screen and get some exercise to refresh.
